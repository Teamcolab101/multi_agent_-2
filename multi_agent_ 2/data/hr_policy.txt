SECTION 1: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES
--------------------------------
1.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
ACME Corporation is committed to equal opportunity employment. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, or any other characteristic protected by law.

1.2 Work Hours
Standard work hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Flexible work arrangements may be approved by department managers. Core hours (10 AM - 4 PM) must be maintained for team collaboration.

1.3 Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week after completing 6 months of employment. Remote work requires manager approval and maintaining productivity standards. Full-remote arrangements require VP-level approval.

1.4 Probation Period
New employees undergo a 90-day probation period. During this time, performance will be closely monitored. At the end of probation, a formal review determines continuation of employment.

SECTION 2: LEAVE MANAGEMENT POLICY
--------------------------------------
2.1 Annual/Casual Leave
- All full-time employees are entitled to 21 days of paid annual leave per calendar year
- Leave accrues at 1.75 days per month
- Maximum carry-forward: 10 days to the next calendar year
- Leave encashment is allowed for up to 15 days at end of year
- Employees must apply for leave at least 3 days in advance (except emergencies)

2.2 Sick Leave
- 12 days of paid sick leave per year
- Medical certificate required for sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Sick leave does not carry forward to next year
- Sick leave cannot be encashed

2.3 Maternity Leave
- 26 weeks (182 days) of paid maternity leave for female employees
- Applicable for first two children
- Can be availed 8 weeks before expected delivery date
- Adoption leave: 12 weeks for adopting a child below 3 months

2.4 Paternity Leave
- 10 days of paid paternity leave
- Must be availed within 3 months of childbirth/adoption
- Can be taken in one stretch or split across two periods

2.5 Bereavement Leave
- 5 days for immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings)
- 3 days for extended family (grandparents, in-laws)
- Additional unpaid leave may be granted on case-by-case basis

2.6 Public Holidays
- 12 mandatory public holidays per year
- 2 optional/floating holidays (employee's choice from approved list)
- Holiday list published at start of each calendar year

2.7 Leave Without Pay (LWP)
- Available when all paid leaves are exhausted
- Requires HR and manager approval
- Maximum continuous LWP: 30 days; beyond that requires special approval

2.8 Leave Application Process
- Apply through HR portal (hr.acmecorp.com/leave)
- Manager must approve/reject within 2 business days
- HR will confirm and update records
- Emergency leave: notify manager via phone/email, apply formally within 24 hours

SECTION 3: COMPENSATION & BENEFITS
--------------------------------------
3.1 Salary Structure
- Base salary paid on last working day of each month
- Annual increment cycle: April 1st each year
- Performance-linked bonus paid in Q1 of following year
- Variable pay: 0-20% of CTC based on performance rating

3.2 Health Insurance
- Group medical insurance for employee + spouse + 2 children + parents
- Sum insured: INR 5,00,000 per family per year
- Pre-existing conditions covered after 2-year waiting period
- Dental and vision: INR 15,000 per year (reimbursement basis)

3.3 Provident Fund
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Employer contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Voluntary PF contribution allowed up to 100% of basic

3.4 Other Benefits
- Gratuity as per Payment of Gratuity Act
- ESOP program for senior employees (Band 5 and above)
- LTA (Leave Travel Allowance): 1 month basic salary per year
- Education assistance: up to INR 50,000 per year (pre-approved courses)

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------
4.1 Performance Review Cycle
- Mid-year review: July (developmental feedback)
- Annual review: March (rating and increment)
- Ratings scale: 1 (Below Expectations) to 5 (Outstanding)
- Rating distribution follows bell curve guidelines

4.2 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Triggered for employees rated 1 or 2 consecutively
- Duration: 60-90 days with specific measurable goals
- Regular check-ins with manager and HR
- Successful completion: normal employment continues
- Failed PIP: may lead to separation

4.3 Recognition Programs
- Spot awards: Up to INR 10,000 (manager-nominated)
- Quarterly star awards: INR 25,000 + trophy
- Annual excellence awards: INR 1,00,000 + trip
- Long service awards: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 year milestones

SECTION 5: CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------------------------
5.1 Professional Behavior
All employees must maintain professional conduct including:
- Treating colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect
- Maintaining confidentiality of company information
- Avoiding conflicts of interest
- Adhering to anti-bribery and corruption policies

5.2 Dress Code
- Business casual Monday through Thursday
- Casual Friday (smart casual, no torn jeans or offensive graphics)
- Formal attire required for client meetings and official events

5.3 Social Media Policy
- Do not share confidential company information on social media
- Personal opinions must be clearly stated as such, not company views
- Positive representation of company culture is encouraged

5.4 Disciplinary Process
- Verbal warning → Written warning → Final warning → Termination
- Gross misconduct may result in immediate termination
- Employee has right to respond at each stage

SECTION 6: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
--------------------------------------
6.1 Grievance Process
- Step 1: Raise concern with immediate manager
- Step 2: Escalate to HR Business Partner
- Step 3: HR Director review
- Step 4: Employee Ombudsman (independent)
- Response timeline: Acknowledgment within 2 days, Resolution within 15 days

6.2 POSH (Prevention of Sexual Harassment)
- Zero tolerance policy for sexual harassment
- Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) constituted
- Complaint to be filed within 90 days of incident
- Inquiry to be completed within 90 days
- Strict confidentiality maintained throughout

SECTION 7: SEPARATION POLICY
--------------------------------------
7.1 Resignation
- Notice period: 30 days (Band 1-3), 60 days (Band 4-5), 90 days (Band 6+)
- Notice buyout: allowed with manager and HR approval
- Handover of responsibilities is mandatory
- Full and final settlement within 45 days of last working day

7.2 Relieving Process
- Submit resignation to manager
- HR initiates exit formalities
- Exit interview mandatory
- Return company assets before last day
- Relieving letter issued after clearance
